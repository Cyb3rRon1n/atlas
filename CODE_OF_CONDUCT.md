# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Atlas project is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and collaborative environment for everyone.

Atlas exists because people enjoy building, learning, and improving self-hosted infrastructure together. Whether you are a beginner setting up your first server or an experienced administrator contributing advanced ideas, your participation is valued.

We are committed to making participation in this project a positive experience for everyone.

---

# Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

* Be respectful and constructive
* Welcome different perspectives and experience levels
* Provide helpful feedback
* Focus discussions on technical improvements
* Explain disagreements professionally
* Help others learn and grow

Examples of positive contributions include:

* Improving documentation
* Sharing knowledge
* Helping troubleshoot issues
* Providing thoughtful reviews
* Suggesting improvements

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

* Harassment or personal attacks
* Discriminatory language
* Deliberate disruption of discussions
* Publishing private information without permission
* Trolling or intentionally hostile behavior
* Dismissing others without constructive explanation

Technical disagreements are expected and healthy. They should remain focused on ideas, implementations, and outcomes—not individuals.

---

# Technical Discussions

Atlas is a technical project, and contributors will sometimes disagree about:

* Architecture decisions
* Technology choices
* Implementation approaches
* Automation strategies

These discussions should be approached with curiosity and respect.

A good technical discussion asks:

> "What problem are we solving, and what solution best supports the project's goals?"

---

# Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

* Keeping discussions productive
* Reviewing contributions fairly
* Addressing unacceptable behavior
* Protecting the project's goals and values

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this code of conduct or the project's mission.

---

# Reporting Issues

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, please report it privately to the project maintainers.

For security-related issues, please follow the instructions in `SECURITY.md`.

For general community concerns, open a discussion or issue when appropriate.

---

# Our Philosophy

Atlas is built around collaboration.

The best infrastructure projects are not created by one person—they are created by communities sharing knowledge and improving ideas together.

Our goal is simple:

> Build useful tools. Share knowledge. Help each other succeed.

Thank you for helping make Atlas a welcoming project.
